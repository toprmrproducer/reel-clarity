Frequently Asked Questions

Is my video uploaded anywhere?

No. ReelClarity runs entirely in your browser. Your video is read locally, processed locally, and the enhanced result stays on your device until you download it.

What does the 5-second preview do?

Before processing your full video, ReelClarity enhances just the first 5 seconds and shows it side by side against the original, so you can judge the quality and see roughly how long the full run will take on your specific device, before committing to the whole file.

What is the difference between AI mode and fallback mode?

ReelClarity first tries a real on-device AI super-resolution model (ESRGAN) to enhance each frame. If your device is too slow to keep the AI model running faster than the video plays back, it automatically switches to a fast, high-quality resample-plus-sharpen pass instead, so you always get a real result rather than an endless wait. The result is always labeled with whichever mode actually ran.

Does the enhanced video keep the original audio?

Not currently. The output focuses purely on the enhanced video frames; audio is not carried over to the enhanced file.

What file format do I get back?

The enhanced video is produced as a WebM file using your browser's built-in MediaRecorder, which plays natively in modern browsers and most video players.

Why does a long or high-resolution video take a while?

Every frame is being enhanced individually on your own device using either an AI model or a high-quality resample pass, with no server doing the heavy lifting. Longer or higher-resolution videos simply mean more frames to process, which takes more time and memory locally. While you wait, there is a quick game of Snake on the tool page to keep you busy.

Is there a file size or length limit?

There's no hard limit enforced by the tool, but very long or very high-resolution videos may use significant memory since everything runs on your device. If your browser becomes unresponsive, try a shorter clip or a lower upscale factor.

Every enhancement runs locally in your browser. Nothing you upload is ever sent to a server.
