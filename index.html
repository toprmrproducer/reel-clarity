Sharpen and upscale your video's resolution right in your browser — nothing is ever uploaded anywhere. Choose a file below and ReelClarity will first process a5-second preview and show it side by sideagainst the original, so you can judge the quality and see how long a full run would take on your device before committing to the whole video.

This tool tries to run a real on-device AI super-resolution model (ESRGAN) frame-by-frame: each video frame is drawn to a hidden canvas, passed through the model to sharpen detail and enhance resolution, then re-encoded into a new video using your browser's built-inMediaRecorder. Per-frame ML upscaling is heavy, so if your device is too slow to process frames faster than the video plays back, ReelClarity automatically falls back to afast sharpness-enhanced upscale(high-quality canvas resampling plus an unsharp-mask sharpening pass) so you still get a real, usable result instead of waiting forever. Whichever mode ran is labeled clearly on the result so you always know what you got.

How it works: your browser decodes the video with a hidden<video>element, seeks through it frame by frame, draws each frame to a canvas, enhances that frame (either through the on-device ESRGAN model or a high-quality resample + sharpen pass), and streams the enhanced canvas intoMediaRecorderto produce a new WebM file. The 5-second preview always runs first so you can judge quality side by side and get a real time estimate — extrapolated directly from how long your device took on the preview — before running the full video. Audio is currently not carried over to the enhanced output. Very long or very high-resolution videos may take a while or use significant memory, since everything happens on your device.
